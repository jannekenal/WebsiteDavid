About ME!


Well, where do I begin? To be very honest,
I am extremely uncomfortable writing about myself.
I have decided, therefore, to post the following disclaimer in order to make myself feel better.

*This brief autobiography is not fiction. Nevertheless, any similarity to real life persons or events, though not coincidental; may be construed as such by the reader.


My life's journey (outside the womb) began on September 22nd, 1951.
I should have known things would go off course, as for the next several decades I celebrated my birthday on September 21st.
If I had only checked the calendar in the delivery room!
Don't ask me how it happened, because I don't know.
At age 56, I had to travel to my hometown, Mansfield, Ohio, and get a copy of my birth certificate.and there it was.. September 22nd.

Things seemed to be fairly good as I grew. 3 siblings, and I was clearly the brightest.
We moved around a lot, but always in the general area in or around Mansfield.
At age 8 I was made aware of just how special I was, when my parents bought me an extra set of eyes. Hindsight is 2020, you know?
I thought being called "four eyes" by my schoolmates was an honor, and issued many a thank you. I have since rescinded them.

I spent many summer weeks at my Aunt Lu Lu's farm out near the local airport.
Loved it out there! Of course, there were a few problems.
I would usually come home covered from head to toe in poison ivy. I did become a terrific tree climber; thanks in part to my aunt's very mean Rhode Island red rooster.
I dearly loved her, but failed to see the humor in being chased up a tree by that awful chicken!

The summer of 1963 we moved to Cleveland, where I attended and graduated from Lincoln High School, in 1969.
While spending a week on the farm at age 16, I met my first love, Becky. After getting back to Cleveland, we began exchanging letters.
You know the kind; handwritten on paper, stuffed in an envelope and mailed with a postage stamp?
Funny, though; after a few exchanges, she somehow got me mixed up with someone named "John", and I never heard from her again.


Although pretty introverted, my self confidence began to soar through my last 3 years of high school.
I had matured into a "poster boy" for those old Charles Atlas bodybuilding ads found in the back of comic books.
Of course, at 98 pounds, I was the "before" photo.
I recall with fondness my select status among my gym classmates.
When team captains were selecting the rest of us for their squads, I held tight to the old adage, "they saved the best for last".
Further, when playing softball, I was always positioned in a special place referred to as "ultra" right field!
It was an honor to protect the vehicles in the school parking lot from damage, if anyone happened to blast a 900 foot homer.



To sum up, then, I graduated with a sterling 2.44 GPA, and began pursuing a bigtime career.umm, sweeping floors and taking out trash at a steel plant.
I bounced around some, eventually settling in at a small business that made industrial quality cabinets, benches and material handling equipment.
Funny, it has been a far cry from the career choice recommended for me by school staff- becoming a writer (Yes, I know; if you have read this far, you are probably glad I ignored that advice).
Now I will take a few moments to fill you in on my "religious" background.
This is, after all, a Christian website.
My early youth was spent in a Pentecostal church, as my great aunt and uncle were ministers in the Assemblies of God denomination.
So, I was exposed to some "tongues speaking" and other interesting manifestations of the "gifts of the Spirit".
But honestly, when you are between age 4 and 8, that stuff comes across as scary, and a little nuts! Between age 9, and my late teens I attended the Salvation Army, first in Mansfield, then in Cleveland, after we moved.
By the time I graduated high school, I had left "church" completely. The most important thing to note here is.although both denominations preached the gospel of Christ, I never did get saved.


Ok then, July 14th, 1985.
At this point in time, I had been married, and divorced after 4 years.
I was the single parent of my 9 year old daughter, Adrienne. Now, July 14th was a beautiful, warm Sunday, and I had been invited to Westside Independent Baptist Church.
Though I had no interest in attending, that day was the church picnic, and I was persuaded to bring Adrienne; at least she could have an afternoon of fun at the Metroparks.
Long story short; the pastor preached, I fell under the conviction of the Holy Spirit, and asked Jesus to be my Lord and Savior.
The most important moment of my life! I was baptized 2 weeks later, and joined the church.
Adrienne was saved as well, after a few months.


The rest, as they say, is history.
I gradually became more involved in church activities, like driving the church van.
In 1990, I began studies at the Heritage Baptist Institute, a ministry of the Cleveland Baptist Church.
I graduated in 1993. Keep in mind, this was a small, unaccredited program, but thoroughly Bible based.
At any given time the entire student body might total 50 to 75 students.
So, depending on your point of view, I graduated either first in my class, or third from the bottom, since there were just 3 of us.
I prefer the former.
God has never called me to be an evangelist, missionary or pastor, though I have preached many times over the years as opportunity and need arose.
I was "drafted" into the congregational song leader position at Westside when the pastor discovered I had a nice "bucket" to carry a tune in (that bucket has long since rusted out at the bottom).but my first love, and I believe my calling, is teaching the Bible.
So, here I am, with a website, a blog, and hopefully, my first of several published works.
I thank the reader for slogging through this page, and ".enduring to the end."



*Footnote: Yes, it is true. I have appropriated a bit of "creative license" while writing this autobiography.
I felt it imperative to try and keep the reader awake beyond the second paragraph.
The substance of my story is true, and that's all you need to know.
If you are offended, feel free to send an exorcist my way.
However, I may need to crowdfund the payment, or risk being repossessed.
